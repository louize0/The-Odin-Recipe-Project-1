PHILIPPINE CUISINE

Philippine food is daunting, exciting, and confusing all at the same time. It’s far from its Southeast Asian neighbors —in flavor, execution, and intention. (Not so far, though; there are still similarities.) Even within the country, the cuisines differ. Food in the North is almost worlds away from those in the South. Even Philippine recipes that grace all local dining tables have slight iterations here and there. For example, adobo is different depending on the region you’re eating it in. Heck, it’s even different per household.
